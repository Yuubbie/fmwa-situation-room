About the Ministry

A mandate rooted in policy and protection

To help build a Nigerian society that guarantees equal access to social, economic, and wealth-creation opportunities to all, irrespective of gender, and that places a premium on the protection of the child, the aged, and persons with disabilities.

We focus the attention of key operators in both the private and public sectors on mainstreaming the concerns of these groups into Nigeria's national development process.

Our Mandate

A mandate rooted in policy and protection

The broad mandate of the Ministry is to advise government on gender and children's issues, and issues affecting persons with disabilities and the aged, while initiating policy guidelines and leading the process of gender equality and mainstreaming at both the national and international levels.

Key Responsibilities

Policy Development

Formulate and implement comprehensive policies on gender equality and women's empowerment.

Women's Rights Advocacy

Advocate for women's rights and ensure their active participation in all sectors of society.

Children's Welfare

Coordinate programmes for children's welfare, protection, and development across the nation.

Disability Support

Support the integration and empowerment of persons with disabilities in society.

Elderly Care

Develop and implement strategies for elderly care and support systems.

International Collaboration

Collaborate with international organisations on gender equality and social development issues.

Strategic Focus Areas

National Level

  • Policy development and implementation
  • Inter-ministerial coordination
  • Community engagement programmes
  • Capacity building initiatives
  • Monitoring and evaluation systems

International Level

  • UN Women collaboration
  • Regional partnerships and networks
  • Global best practices adoption
  • International treaty compliance
  • Cross-border initiatives

Legal Framework

  • ✓Constitution of the Federal Republic of Nigeria (1999), fundamental rights and non-discrimination provisions.
  • ✓National Gender Policy (2006), framework for gender mainstreaming.
  • ✓Violence Against Persons (Prohibition) Act (2015), protection against gender-based violence.
  • ✓CEDAW, Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination Against Women.
  • ✓Beijing Platform for Action, global commitment to gender equality.
  • ✓Sustainable Development Goals, particularly Goal 5 on Gender Equality.
